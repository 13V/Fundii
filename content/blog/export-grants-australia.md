# Export Grants for Australian Businesses: EMDG and Beyond (2025–26)

**If your Australian business is ready to go global, export grants are one of the most reliable and generous government funding programs you'll ever access.**

*Export grants in Australia* — led by the **Export Market Development Grant (EMDG)** — exist because the government understands that breaking into international markets is expensive. Market research, trade shows, overseas marketing agencies, IP registration, and sales trips drain cash flow before a single foreign dollar comes back.

The Australian Government wants you to export. Every dollar of foreign revenue you generate is capital imported into the Australian economy. That's why Austrade and state government bodies maintain active, well-funded grant programs specifically to subsidise the cost of going global.

This is the complete 2025–26 guide to Australian export grants: who qualifies, what the money covers, and how to maximise what you can access.

---

## The Heavyweight: Export Market Development Grant (EMDG)

The **Export Market Development Grant (EMDG)**, administered by Austrade, is the flagship export support program for Australian businesses. For serious exporters, it's not just a nice-to-have — it's an essential part of international growth strategy.

### How the EMDG Program Works

EMDG reimburses eligible Australian businesses for **up to 50% of qualifying export promotion expenses**. The program operates on a tiered structure based on your export maturity:

| Tier | Who It's For | Typical Grant Amount |
|------|--------------|---------------------|
| **Tier 1: Ready to Export** | New exporters taking first steps | Up to $40,000/year |
| **Tier 2: Exporting and Expanding** | Active exporters growing existing markets | Up to $80,000/year |
| **Tier 3: Exporting Strategically** | Experienced exporters entering new global markets | Up to $150,000/year |

Over your lifetime in the program, you can access up to **$770,000 across 8 financial years**.

### Who Qualifies for EMDG?

Core eligibility requirements:
- **Australian business:** Must hold an active ABN and operate predominantly in Australia
- **Turnover threshold:** Aggregated annual turnover must be **under $20 million** for Tier 1 and 2 applicants
- **Eligible product or service:** Must be promoting a product substantially manufactured in Australia, or a service that generates a clear net benefit to Australia

### What Expenses Does EMDG Cover?

**Eligible EMDG expenses include:**
- International trade show registration fees, exhibition space, and booth construction
- Return airfares and accommodation for sales/business development trips overseas
- Fees paid to overseas marketing agencies or distributors
- Translating and adapting marketing materials for international markets
- Fees for international IP protection (patent filing, trademark registration in target markets)
- Market research reports from recognised international research providers
- Free samples sent to prospective overseas customers or distributors

**Not eligible under EMDG:**
- Manufacturing costs of the product being exported
- Domestic-market marketing expenses
- Existing agent commissions on current sales (only activities to *develop* new export business)
- GST components of any expense

## How to Lodge Your EMDG Application

EMDG applications are lodged through the **Austrade portal** after the end of the financial year in which the expenses occurred. The general process:

1. **Register on the Austrade portal** and create your business profile
2. **Log eligible expenses throughout the year** as they occur (keep all invoices, receipts, proof of payment, and evidence the work was done)
3. **Submit your annual application** through the portal within the lodgement window (typically August–October each year)
4. **Await Austrade assessment** (typically 3–6 months)
5. **Receive payment** via direct transfer if approved

**Critical compliance:** EMDG is auditable. For every dollar you claim, you must be able to produce the supplier invoice, proof of the bank transfer or payment, and ideally evidence that the activity actually occurred (e.g., photos from the trade show, the marketing agency's deliverable report). No documentation = no payment.

## Austrade's Go Global Toolkit: Beyond the Grant

EMDG is the cash, but Austrade's broader **Go Global Toolkit** offers non-cash support that's equally valuable:

- **Free market intelligence reports** for target countries (regulatory environment, competitive landscape, distribution structures)
- **Introductions to Austrade in-market trade commissioners** globally — in Tokyo, Jakarta, Dubai, London, New York, and 80+ other locations
- **TradeStart advisers** embedded in industry associations and export chambers across Australia, providing subsidised one-on-one export coaching

If you need a warm introduction to a distributor in Singapore or a regulatory specialist in Germany, Austrade's network can often make that happen for free or a fraction of commercial rates.

## State Export Grant Programs: NSW, QLD, and Victoria

Don't limit your search to the federal EMDG. State governments run their own export programs — typically easier to win, with faster turnaround times.

### NSW Export Assistance Grants
NSW Trade and Investment offers grants of **typically $5,000–$10,000** to help NSW businesses:
- Attend international trade shows and expos
- Translate and localise marketing assets into Chinese, Japanese, Korean, or other languages
- Conduct market feasibility studies for entering ASEAN markets
- Register intellectual property in target markets

### Trade and Investment Queensland (TIQ)
TIQ offers grant-backed trade missions, primarily targeting the Asia-Pacific market. If your Queensland business has strong potential in Southeast Asian agri-food, tourism, or resources supply chain sectors, TIQ trade missions provide subsidised delegation participation alongside in-market meetings arranged by TIQ trade commissioners.

### Global Victoria
The Victorian Government's export agency runs regular **funded international trade missions** — taking delegations of Victorian businesses to major markets — and provides matched grant funding for Victorian exporters attending approved international trade shows. Ask Business Victoria specifically about current Global Victoria programs.

## Common EMDG Mistakes That Cost Australian Businesses Money

EMDG audits are thorough. Avoid these frequently made errors:

1. **Claiming expenses without supporting documentation.** Every line item must have: the supplier's invoice, your payment evidence (bank transfer details), and ideally a deliverable report. "I paid $20,000 to an agency in LA" without an invoice gets rejected.

2. **Mixing domestic and international marketing expenses.** If an agency builds you a website that targets both Australian and US customers, only the US-facing portion is eligible. You must maintain clear separation in your accounting.

3. **Missing the lodgement window.** EMDG has a fixed lodgement period each year (typically August–October for the prior financial year's expenses). Missing this window means losing your claim for that year — you cannot carry expenses forward.

4. **Registering too late.** Some EMDG tiers require you to register your export activities *before* incurring expenses (particularly Tier 3 activities). Check with Austrade on whether your planned activities need pre-registration.

## Frequently Asked Questions: Export Grants in Australia

### Can Australian service businesses apply for EMDG export grants?
Yes. You don't need to be physically exporting boxes of product to qualify. Australian service exporters are fully eligible — including architects, engineers, management consultants, software companies, educators, and professional services firms. If you're a SaaS company with subscribers in North America, a law firm doing international work, or a training company running programs in Singapore, you're likely exporting eligible services.

### Are digital products and software eligible for EMDG?
Yes. Software, apps, SaaS subscriptions, online courses, digital content, and other entirely digital products are among the most attractive EMDG claims — they bring foreign revenue into Australia at near-zero marginal cost, making the case for export promotion highly credible.

### Do I need to have export revenue already to access EMDG?
Not for Tier 1 (Ready to Export), which specifically supports businesses in the process of establishing their first export relationships. You do need to demonstrate genuine intent and readiness to export, and have a viable eligible product or service — but you don't need an existing export revenue track record.

### Can I use an EMDG consultant to prepare my application?
Yes. There is an industry of EMDG consultants who help businesses structure and lodge their claims. They typically charge 10–15% of the grant received. For businesses claiming small amounts ($15,000–$40,000), the maths often don't work out in your favour. For larger, complex claims, a specialist can be worth the fee. Alternatively, tools like Grant Base help you structure your own application correctly.

### Can I access EMDG and a state export grant at the same time?
Yes — provided you're claiming different expenses in each program. You cannot claim the same trade show flight in both an EMDG claim and a NSW export assistance grant. But you can claim your trade show registration under EMDG while your in-market marketing agency fees go under a state export grant, for example.
