# Victorian Government Grants for Small Business: 2025–26 Complete Guide

**Victoria has some of the most diverse small business grant programs in Australia — but the window to apply is usually short, and many businesses miss out simply because they weren't prepared.**

*Victorian government grants for small businesses* cover everything from solar energy upgrades to advanced manufacturing investment, startup equity funding, regional expansion grants, and AgTech innovation. The Business Victoria portal is the gateway, but understanding the programs behind it separates the businesses that win funding from those that miss out.

This is the full guide to Victorian government small business grants available in 2025–26: who's eligible, what's available, and how to apply before the funding closes.

## Business Victoria: The Central Hub for Victorian Small Business Funding

The **Business Victoria website** (business.vic.gov.au) is the official starting point for all state government small business support in Victoria. When major grant programs are announced — typically around the May state budget — they are published here first.

However, Business Victoria only shows you programs once they're actively open. Most competitive rounds close within 4–8 weeks of opening. If you're waiting for a program to be announced before you start preparing financial documents and project plans, you'll almost certainly miss it.

The smart move: **get your application documents ready now**, so you can submit within days of a relevant round opening. These include:
- Last 2 years of Profit & Loss statements and balance sheets (accountant-verified)
- Current business plan
- Supplier quotes for any equipment or services the grant will fund
- Proof of ABN and Victorian business registration

## Key Victorian Government Grant Programs for Small Businesses in 2025–26

### Small Business Digital Adaptation Rebate
Victoria's digital adaptation program provides rebates to help small businesses adopt approved digital tools. While the specific iteration of this program shifts with each budget cycle, Victoria has consistently offered rebates (historically around **$1,200**) for small businesses subscribing to approved platforms, including:
- Cloud accounting software (Xero, MYOB, QuickBooks)
- E-commerce and online retail platforms (Shopify, WooCommerce)
- Job management and scheduling systems
- Inventory and supply chain software

Eligibility typically requires fewer than 20 employees, active ABN in Victoria, and a minimum period of trading.

### LaunchVic: Victoria's Startup Engine
**LaunchVic** is the Victorian Government's dedicated startup ecosystem funding agency. Beyond backing incubators and angel networks, they periodically release direct grants for:
- Early-stage founders, particularly those from underrepresented demographics
- AgTech and HealthTech ventures
- Startups developing technology with strong export potential
- Programs helping Victorian startups access international capital

LaunchVic also funds accelerator programs that provide a combination of investment readiness training, mentorship, and direct grant funding.

### Regional Development Victoria Grants
If your business is based in regional Victoria — Geelong, Ballarat, Bendigo, Horsham, Mildura, Gippsland, or further afield — you access a separate, less competitive pool of funding through **Regional Development Victoria (RDV)**.

RDV typically co-funds large business expansion and infrastructure projects on the condition that you commit to creating a defined number of permanent local jobs. Grants have historically ranged from **$50,000 to several million dollars** for projects that significantly drive regional economic activity.

## Manufacturing Grants for Victorian Businesses

Victoria positions itself as Australia's manufacturing heartland, and the state government backs this claim with significant targeted investment.

### Made in Victoria Manufacturing Grants
This program co-funds Victorian manufacturers in adopting advanced manufacturing technology. Eligible technology upgrades include:
- Industrial robotics and automated assembly lines
- AI-driven quality control systems
- 3D printing and additive manufacturing
- Advanced CNC machining centres
- Digital twin and smart factory infrastructure

Grants are matched, meaning for every eligible dollar your business invests, the government contributes a share. Past rounds have offered anywhere from **$50,000 to $500,000+** in matched funding. Competition is fierce — a strong, specific project description with detailed budgets is essential.

## Sustainability Grants: Solar, Energy Efficiency, and Net Zero for Victorian Businesses

Victoria leads Australia in small business sustainability funding. If you're running a business with high energy costs, these programs can deliver immediate bottom-line improvements.

### Solar for Business Program
Victoria's **Solar for Business Program** provides rebates covering **up to 50% of installation costs** for rooftop solar systems, historically capped around $3,500 per application (with higher amounts available for larger commercial systems).

If you operate a warehouse, mechanic workshop, manufacturing facility, or any other high-consumption commercial premises, even a small solar rebate can deliver years of compounding energy cost savings.

### Energy Efficiency Grants for Business
For businesses undertaking larger energy efficiency overhauls — replacing industrial HVAC, upgrading refrigeration systems, or modernising large-scale lighting — Victoria periodically offers specific round-based grants to co-fund these capital expenditures.

These grants require an energy audit (to document current consumption and projected savings) before application, so don't wait until a round opens to commission the audit.

## Agriculture Victoria: Grant Programs for Primary Producers

Victoria's agricultural sector — dairy, horticulture, viticulture, and livestock — is one of the state's most significant economic contributors. Agriculture Victoria manages a portfolio of programs for Victorian primary producers:

- **Biosecurity infrastructure upgrades:** Grants and subsidised access for producers implementing enhanced biosecurity measures, particularly those in high-risk pest and disease areas.
- **AgTech adoption:** Funding for smart irrigation systems, precision livestock monitoring, autonomous harvesting equipment, and remote sensing tools.
- **Food processing and value-adding:** Grants for producers building on-farm or local processing capability — turning raw milk into cheese, raw grapes into bottled wine, or fresh produce into packaged meals — to capture higher margins.

## Regional vs. Metro Eligibility: A Critical Distinction

When applying for Victorian small business grants, **your postcode matters enormously**.

Victorian businesses registered in Melbourne metropolitan councils (including all inner and middle-ring suburbs) access the general state-wide funding pool. The competition here is intense — tens of thousands of eligible businesses apply.

Businesses registered in **designated regional Victoria postcodes** access a separate, specifically ring-fenced pool of funding with:
- Significantly less competition
- Higher per-business grant amounts
- A stronger weighting given to regional employment outcomes

If your business has operations in both metro Melbourne and regional Victoria (e.g., a head office in CBD, plus a manufacturing facility in Ballarat), structure your application to emphasise the regional economic impact to maximise your eligibility for regional-tier funding.

## Frequently Asked Questions: Victorian Government Grants for Small Business

### Does my business need to be registered in Victoria to apply for Victorian government grants?
Yes. To access Business Victoria state funding, your business must be primarily based in Victoria. Your ABN registration address (as listed on the Australian Business Register) should reflect your Victorian location. If your ABN is registered in NSW but you have staff in Victoria, you will typically not be considered a Victorian business for grant purposes.

### Can a Victorian business with interstate customers or revenue still apply for Victorian grants?
Yes. Victoria-based businesses that sell goods or services interstate or internationally are fully eligible for Victorian government grants — in fact, export revenue is viewed favourably by assessors as it demonstrates economic activity that brings external money into Victoria.

### How do I prove job creation commitments for Victorian regional grants?
Regional Job Creation grants require a formal commitment in the funding agreement to employing a specific number of workers by a defined date. The roles must be genuine, permanent positions — not contractors or existing roles repackaged as "new" jobs. You will be audited on this for the life of the grant and in subsequent years.

### What are the typical grant amounts available through Business Victoria?
This varies dramatically by program. Small digital adaptation rebates: around $1,200. Regional Business Development grants: $10,000 to $100,000. Made in Victoria Manufacturing grants: $50,000 to $500,000+. LaunchVic startup funding: $20,000 to $250,000+ (typically through programs rather than direct grants). Large infrastructure projects: $1M+.

### Can my Victorian business apply for both state and federal government grants?
Yes — and this is common practice. The key rule is no double-dipping: you cannot claim the same expense under two different grant programs. But different components of a broader business growth project can often be funded through different grants simultaneously.
