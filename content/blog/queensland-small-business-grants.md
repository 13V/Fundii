## Advance Queensland: The State's Innovation Funding Powerhouse

**Advance Queensland** is the Queensland Government's flagship initiative for innovation and economic development. It's not a single program — it's a suite of funding streams, all aimed at growing high-value industries and creating skilled jobs in Queensland.

### Advance Queensland Innovation Program
If your business is developing a genuinely new product, service, or technology that has commercial potential and will create skilled jobs in Queensland, the Innovation Program is your primary target.

Grant amounts under this program have historically ranged from **$50,000 to $300,000+** in matched funding, depending on project scale and alignment with Queensland's economic priorities. The program typically requires:
- A Queensland-registered company with a valid ABN
- Evidence of technical or commercial innovation
- A clear path to market
- Commitment to employing local workers

The key to winning Innovation Program funding is demonstrating **economic additionality** — that is, the project wouldn't happen, or wouldn't happen in Queensland, without government support. Assessors score heavily on this.

### Ignite Ideas Fund
Part of the Advance Queensland family, the **Ignite Ideas Fund** specifically targets early-stage product and service innovation, with grants typically in the **$25,000–$75,000** range. This is more accessible for smaller businesses and startups that can't yet demonstrate the scale required for larger Innovation Program funding.

## Queensland Regional Business Grants

Queensland is geographically enormous — and the government actively invests in pulling economic activity out of Brisbane and into regional areas. If your business is based or planning to expand outside the south-east corner, you access a less competitive, dedicated regional funding tier.

**Common regional grant categories in Queensland:**

- **Regional Economic Development Grants:** Co-fund expansion projects — new facilities, large equipment purchases, workforce scale-ups — for businesses in regional cities and towns. Available in areas like Townsville, Cairns, Rockhampton, Mackay, and rural Queensland.

- **Freight and Logistics Subsidies:** Periodic programs offsetting the significant cost of transporting goods between regional Queensland and major ports or markets — critical for manufacturers and producers in isolated areas.

- **Remote Area Business Incentives:** For businesses operating in genuinely remote Queensland (e.g., far western Queensland, Cape York, Torres Strait), specialist support packages exist that are not available to businesses in regional centres.

## Industry-Specific Queensland Business Grants

### Tourism Grants in Queensland (A Major Opportunity)

Tourism is one of Queensland's largest economic sectors — from the Great Barrier Reef to the Gold Coast to the Daintree. The Queensland Government invests significantly in helping tourism operators upgrade facilities and attract high-value visitors.

Watch for **Tourism Experience Development Grants**, which have historically provided co-funding of **$10,000 to $100,000** for tourism businesses to:
- Build or upgrade accommodation and activity facilities
- Develop new cultural, adventure, or nature tourism products
- Upgrade marine vessels for reef or island tours
- Develop accessibility infrastructure for tourism sites

Post-COVID, the state has maintained heightened tourism support to rebuild domestic and international visitor numbers. If you're in hospitality, attractions, or accommodation, this is one of the best funding categories available to you.

### Agriculture: QDAF Programs
The **Queensland Department of Agriculture and Fisheries (QDAF)** manages an extensive range of agricultural funding:

- **Drought Preparation and Recovery:** Grants and subsidised loans for producers preparing for or recovering from drought events. Includes funding for water storage infrastructure, destocking assistance, and pasture renovation.
- **AgTech Adoption Vouchers:** Funding to trial and implement precision agriculture technology — smart sensors, irrigation management systems, drone monitoring, automated harvesting.
- **Value-Add Manufacturing:** Grants helping primary producers add processing capability on-farm to improve margins (e.g., a macadamia grower building a roasting and packaging facility).

### QLD Manufacturing and Industry Investment
Queensland's manufacturing sector benefits from round-based investment programs tied to the state's broader economic development agenda. Programs targeting **Queensland-made advanced manufacturing** — robotics, automation, AI-integrated production systems — regularly attract co-investment grants alongside significant federal support through AusIndustry.

## Remote and Indigenous Business Grants in Queensland

Queensland has dedicated grant structures for Indigenous-owned enterprises and businesses operating in remote areas. These programs address the unique barriers faced by First Nations businesses and remote operators:

- Grants for First Nations businesses to access technical and business advisory services
- Funding for capacity building programs that help Indigenous businesses tender for larger government contracts
- Infrastructure and connectivity grants for businesses in areas with limited access to markets

If you're an Indigenous business owner or operate in a remote Queensland community, contact the **Queensland Department of Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander Partnerships** for the most current funding available specifically to you.

## How to Write a Winning Queensland Small Business Grant Application

QLD assessors look for the same core things as every other state — but there are a few Queensland-specific tips worth knowing:

1. **Reference Queensland's economic priorities explicitly:** Every state budget sets priority sectors. In recent years, QLD has emphasised renewable energy, tourism recovery, defence industry supply chains, and resources sector diversification. Read the budget papers and mirror their language.

2. **Be very specific about regional employment outcomes:** Even if you're in a regional centre rather than a fully remote area, any commitment to new jobs in regional QLD scores significantly. Quantify exactly: how many roles, what salary range, what level of permanency.

3. **Use Business Queensland's self-assessment tools:** The portal has pre-screening questionnaires for many programs. Use them before investing time writing — they tell you within minutes whether you meet basic eligibility.

## Frequently Asked Questions: Queensland Small Business Grants

### How long does a Queensland government grant take to assess?
It varies significantly by program size. Simple digital adaptation rebates: 3–6 weeks. Standard competitive small business grants ($10,000–$50,000): typically 8–12 weeks. Larger Advance Queensland innovation grants: 3–6 months for assessment, with milestone-based payment schedules after approval. Always check the expected timeline noted in each program's guidelines.

### Are there government grants for the hospitality sector in Queensland?
Hospitality businesses face limited access to direct "cash to run your cafe" grants — those don't really exist. But QLD hospitality businesses frequently qualify for: energy efficiency upgrades (commercial refrigeration, HVAC), digital adaptation grants (online booking systems, POS software), and tourism experience development grants if they are part of the broader tourism economy.

### Do I need a grant writer or consultant for Queensland grant applications?
For small straightforward programs (under $25,000), a capable business owner can self-apply. For major Advance Queensland Innovation Program applications or larger regional development grants, having someone experienced in QLD government grant language review your application before submission is strongly recommended. Alternatively, AI tools like Grant Base can help draft and structure your application.

### Can a Queensland business apply for federal grants at the same time?
Yes — and many successful businesses run parallel applications against both state and federal programs. This is not double-dipping, provided the expenses claimed are different in each application. For example, claiming R&D salaries under the R&D Tax Incentive while simultaneously applying for an Advance Queensland market development grant.
