## Service NSW Business Bureau: Your Starting Point for NSW Small Business Grants

Before you search for individual grant programs, set up your presence on the **Service NSW Business Bureau** portal at business.gov.au/au/state-and-territory/nsw.

Service NSW has increasingly centralised its small business support services. In practice, this means:
- Most NSW state grant applications are now submitted through a unified Service NSW account
- Your ABN, business details, and banking information need to be verified and linked in advance
- Some grant programs require your business to be "pre-registered" in the portal before a round opens

**Do this now, not when a grant opens.** Applicants who scramble to set up their portal account during a competitive grant round often miss the deadline.

## Key NSW Government Grant Programs for Small Businesses in 2025–26

The specific names of NSW grant programs shift each financial year as the state budget is refreshed. But the *categories* remain reliably consistent. Here's what to look for:

### NSW Digital Solutions Program (ASBAS)
The **Australian Small Business Advisory Services (ASBAS) Digital Solutions** program is jointly funded by the federal and NSW state governments. It provides eligible small businesses with access to heavily subsidised digital advisory support — typically valued at $3,000–$6,000 — for:
- Website development and e-commerce setup
- Cybersecurity assessments and implementation
- Cloud accounting software migration (Xero, MYOB, QuickBooks)
- Social media and digital marketing strategy
- Inventory and supply chain software implementation

Eligibility: businesses with fewer than 20 employees, operating for at least 6 months.

### NSW Export Assistance Grants
If your NSW business is ready to enter an international market, the state regularly offers export assistance programs providing grants of **up to $10,000** to help pay for:
- International trade show registration and travel
- Export market research reports
- Translation and localisation of marketing materials for Asian markets
- Regulatory compliance consulting for overseas market entry

These pair extremely well with the federal **Export Market Development Grant (EMDG)** program — and you can hold both simultaneously, provided you're not claiming the exact same expenses twice.

### SafeWork NSW Small Business Rebates
For cafes, tradies, retailers, and any other business with workplace health and safety obligations, SafeWork NSW provides rebates of **up to $1,000** for purchasing approved safety equipment, such as:
- Anti-fatigue matting
- Ergonomic lifting equipment
- Dust extraction and ventilation improvements
- Safer manual handling equipment

The rebate is available to NSW businesses with fewer than 50 employees. It's low paperwork, fast to receive, and widely underutilised.

## Industry-Specific NSW Government Grants

New South Wales doesn't just offer general business grants — it heavily invests in specific industries that drive state growth.

### Create NSW (Creative Industries)
**Create NSW** manages the state government's arts and cultural funding. For commercially-oriented creative businesses (production companies, game developers, film studios, design agencies), Create NSW offers:
- Project development grants
- Export market development for creative content
- First Nations cultural projects
- Screen content production incentives (for film and TV)

NSW is home to Australia's largest screen production industry — if you're in this space, Create NSW funding is significantly accessible.

### NSW Department of Primary Industries (Agriculture)
NSW DPI funds a wide range of agricultural business and research programs:
- **Regional Job Creation Fund:** Co-funding for regional agribusinesses and manufacturers expanding operations and creating new permanent local jobs — often covering 20–50% of eligible project costs
- **AgTech adoption support:** Grants and vouchers for primary producers implementing precision agriculture technology
- **Drought preparedness and disaster recovery:** Direct financial assistance for farmers and rural businesses affected by drought, flood, or bushfire

### Jobs and Economy — Western Sydney
If you're located in or willing to plant operations in Western Sydney, the NSW Government has ring-fenced significant investment to fund the region's economic development. Programs targeting tech businesses, advanced manufacturing, and logistics in Western Sydney routinely offer larger grant amounts than metro-wide programs.

## How to Write a Winning NSW Government Grant Application

NSW-specific application tips that applicants who miss funding rarely know:

**1. Align explicitly with NSW Budget Priorities**
Every NSW Budget names specific economic priorities for the year. In recent years these have included Western Sydney investment, net-zero transition, digital infrastructure, and regional economic development. Assessors reward applications that explicitly reference and align with these stated priorities. Read the budget papers.

**2. Match ABN details to bank account names exactly**
The Service NSW portal system auto-validates your bank account name against your registered business name. A mismatch (e.g., trading name vs. legal entity name) will hold up or reject your application. Get this right before submission.

**3. Include a project Gantt chart or milestone table**
NSW business grant applications score well when you provide a clear timeline — even a basic table showing Month 1, Month 2, Month 3 activities and what's happening at each stage. It demonstrates project management capability.

## Frequently Asked Questions: NSW Government Grants for Small Business

### Can I apply for federal government grants and NSW state grants at the same time?
Yes. In fact, stacking federal and state grants strategically is common practice among experienced grant applicants in NSW. What you cannot do is claim the exact same project expense simultaneously in both a state and a federal grant application. Different expenses from the same project can be split across programs.

### What is the difference between an NSW government grant and a rebate?
A **grant** is typically awarded before a project begins and provides upfront funding to make the project happen. A **rebate** requires you to incur the eligible expense first — out of your own pocket — and then reimburse you a portion after you've submitted proof of purchase. SafeWork NSW's safety rebate is an example of the rebate model.

### Are there NSW government grants specifically for starting a new business?
Direct cash grants purely for business startup are extremely rare. NSW government funding generally targets existing businesses with at least 6–12 months of trading. New businesses are better served by incubator programs, subsidised advisory services (like ASBAS Digital Solutions), or federal government programs like the New Business Assistance with NEIS scheme for sole traders.

### Do I need an accountant to help me apply for NSW government grants?
For smaller grants (under $25,000), you generally don't need professional help beyond your existing accountant. For larger competitive programs — particularly regional job creation funds or large-scale manufacturing support — having an experienced grants writer or adviser review your application before submission significantly improves outcomes.

### How competitive are NSW small business grants?
Competition varies significantly by program. Popular digital adoption rebates (low dollar amount, simple paperwork) receive high application volumes. Specialised regional or sector-specific grants often have far fewer applicants relative to the funding pool. Your best odds are in programs aligned specifically to your industry and location.
