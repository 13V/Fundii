# Agriculture Grants in Australia: Funding for Farmers and Agribusiness (2025–26)

**If you farm in Australia, there's a very good chance the government will co-fund your next major investment — and a very good chance you don't know it.**

*Agriculture grants in Australia* span drought resilience, AgTech adoption, value-adding, biosecurity, water efficiency, organic certification, and young farmer support programs. Both federal and state governments view primary production as a national security issue, not just an economic one — meaning they fund agricultural business improvement at a scale most other sectors never see.

## Federal Agriculture Grants for Australian Farmers: The Big Programs

The federal government manages the large structural programs that underpin the long-term resilience of Australian agriculture.

### Farm Household Allowance (FHA)
The **Farm Household Allowance** is an income support payment for farmers and their partners who are experiencing financial hardship. While it's more of a safety net than a growth grant, FHA is specifically designed to keep farming families viable during restructuring or recovery periods — allowing you to focus on business decisions without the acute pressure of no income.

FHA is means-tested and assessed by Services Australia. It covers farm businesses across all agricultural sectors.

### Future Drought Fund: $5 Billion for Drought Resilience
The **Future Drought Fund** is one of the most significant federal commitments to Australian agriculture in decades. This $5 billion fund doesn't just react to drought — it proactively funds investment in drought resilience before the dry hits.

The Fund distributes through several streams:
- **Drought Resilience Self-Assessment:** Free assessments helping farmers identify vulnerabilities and then access funding for improvement plans
- **Drought Resilience R&D:** Grants for research and adoption of drought-adapted crop varieties, soil moisture monitoring, and climate-smart farming practices
- **Infrastructure Co-Investment:** Grants for building or upgrading water storage, fodder storage, and farm infrastructure specifically designed to handle extended dry periods

If your operation is in a climate-vulnerable location, the Future Drought Fund should be a standing item on your grant application calendar.

### Biosecurity Grants for Australian Agribusinesses
With global trade volumes at record levels, protecting Australian agriculture from exotic pests and diseases is a top-tier government priority. Programs managed by the **Department of Agriculture, Fisheries and Forestry (DAFF)** regularly fund:
- Implementation of advanced biosecurity management systems for large agribusinesses
- Industry-wide traceability systems (for meat, dairy, seafood, and horticulture)
- Physical infrastructure upgrades to reduce pest and disease entry risk on farm

These grants tend to be larger in scale and often accessed through industry bodies rather than individual producers applying directly — engage your relevant peak body to understand what's available in your sector.

## State Agriculture Grants for Australian Farmers: Everyday Upgrades

While the federal government handles strategic programs, state agriculture departments (NSW DPI, Agriculture Victoria, QDAF, PIRSA, DPIRD) fund the practical, day-to-day business upgrades. These are the grants most Australian farmers are actually eligible for.

### Drought and Disaster Recovery (All States)
Every state maintains a rapid-response funding mechanism for when drought, flood, bushfire, or cyclone events hit. These typically include:
- **Direct cash grants** for restocking livestock or replacing fencing destroyed by floods or fires
- **Low-interest emergency loans** for bridging cash flow gaps during drought
- **Freight subsidies** covering the cost of moving fodder or livestock to/from affected areas
- **Mental health and advisory services** for farm families under extreme stress

When a disaster event is declared in your region, act quickly — most recovery assistance programs are time-limited and operate on a first-come, first-served basis.

### AgTech and Innovation Adoption Grants
States want farmers off paper ledgers and onto precision agriculture platforms. Both state and federal programs regularly offer grants for AgTech adoption:

- **Drone-based crop mapping and yield monitoring** (replacing expensive helicopter monitoring)
- **Smart RFID livestock tracking** and weight monitoring systems
- **Automated irrigation systems** triggered by soil moisture sensors (reducing water usage by 30–50% at some operations)
- **Variable rate fertiliser and chemical application** using GPS-guided precision spreaders

Grant amounts for individual AgTech adoption typically range from **$5,000 to $30,000** for SME agricultural businesses, with higher amounts available for research partnerships.

### Value-Adding Manufacturing Grants
Governments consistently want primary producers to capture more of the value chain. Grants exist specifically to help farmers build on-farm processing capability, moving from low-margin raw commodity sales to higher-margin finished product sales:

- A sheep station adding a small wool-scouring and spinning facility
- A macadamia orchard adding a roasting and packaging line
- A dairy farm building a small-batch cheese-making facility
- A fruit grower building a processing and bottling plant for premium juices

State programs typically offer **25–50% co-funding** for eligible infrastructure and equipment costs in these projects.

## CSIRO Kick-Start: Matched Funding for Agricultural Innovation

If your agribusiness is working on genuinely novel agricultural technology — not just adopting existing tools, but *developing* something new — the **CSIRO Kick-Start program** is designed for you.

Available to Australian SMEs and startups, the program provides **up to $50,000 in 1:1 matched funding** to engage a CSIRO scientist or access CSIRO facilities for R&D collaboration. Recent agricultural Kick-Start projects have included:
- AI systems for early-stage pest and disease detection in broadacre crops
- New feed formulations to reduce methane emissions in beef and dairy cattle
- Novel soil biology treatments to improve plant pathogen resistance
- Gene expression research for drought-tolerant plant varieties

This program is administered directly by CSIRO and requires your business to be incorporated (Pty Ltd). **[Assess your eligibility →](https://grantbase.com.au/quiz)**

## Water and Irrigation Efficiency Grants

Water is the most constrained resource in Australian agriculture. Investment in efficient water infrastructure delivers compounding returns across the life of the asset — and government grants exist to help fund the upgrade.

Programs tied to the **Murray-Darling Basin Plan** and various state water departments fund:
- Conversion from flood or sprinkler irrigation to high-efficiency drip systems
- Installation of central pivot irrigators and linear move systems
- Telemetry and remote monitoring for water entitlement management
- Dam lining and water storage sealing works

The catch: you must demonstrate exactly how many megalitres of water the upgraded system will save annually compared to the previous system. Get an irrigation consultant to prepare this analysis — it's a mandatory part of the grant application.

## Young Farmer and Farm Succession Grants

The average age of an Australian farmer is rising every year — a demographic challenge the government is actively working to reverse through targeted funding.

Programs available for young farmers and succession planning include:
- **Young Farmer Business Bootcamps:** State-funded intensive programs combining business skills training with a direct cash grant component
- **First Block Access:** Some state programs offer subsidised loan components to help young farmers access their first parcel of farming land
- **Farm Succession Planning Support:** Subsidised legal and financial advisory services to help families structure intergenerational farm ownership transitions tax-efficiently

These programs are particularly active in South Australia, Queensland, and Victoria. Contact your state's agriculture department directly for what's currently running.

## Organic Certification and Sustainable Agriculture Grants

Consumer demand for certified organic and sustainably produced food is growing rapidly — but the economics of transition are brutal. The 3–5 year conversion process to certified organic typically reduces yields significantly before the premium pricing kicks in.

Government programs to watch:
- **Sustainable Farming Transition Grants:** Some state programs have offered co-funding to offset income lost during transition to organic certification
- **Carbon Farming Initiatives:** Under the Australian Carbon Credit Unit (ACCU) Scheme, farmers can earn income from sequestering carbon or reducing methane emissions — effectively a payment for environmental sustainability
- **Tree-Planting and Revegetation Grants:** Programs through the National Landcare Program and state equivalents fund commercial tree planting on farms — both for carbon sequestration and biodiversity outcomes

## Frequently Asked Questions: Agriculture Grants in Australia

### Can hobby farms apply for Australian agriculture grants?
Generally, no. Virtually all state and federal agriculture grants require you to be a legitimate **Primary Producer** — a formal tax classification by the ATO requiring that farming represents your main business activity and source of income. Casual hobby farms (a few chickens, a small market garden) do not qualify for commercial production grants.

### Do I need to own the farming land to apply for agriculture grants?
Not always. Share farmers and long-term lessees are eligible for many equipment, AgTech, and digital adoption grants — provided they have a registered ABN, meet primary producer criteria, and have the landowner's permission for any infrastructure projects. For grants funding permanent infrastructure (dams, shedding, irrigation infrastructure), the landowner typically needs to be a co-applicant.

### Are agriculture grants available for standard equipment like tractors?
Standard equipment replacement (an old tractor for a newer-but-equivalent tractor) is typically classified as "business as usual" expenditure and is not eligible. Grants fund innovation and improvement: upgrading to an autonomous GPS-guided tractor, adopting precision application equipment, or implementing technology your operation never had before. The test is whether the project makes your farm meaningfully more efficient, sustainable, or productive in a new way.

### What's the difference between a farm loan and an agriculture grant?
An agriculture grant is non-repayable government funding — you keep the money provided you spend it as approved. An agriculture loan (such as those offered through the Regional Investment Corporation's Farm Investment Loan or drought loans) must be repaid, typically at concessional interest rates. Many programs combine both — a grant component for eligible capital costs and a loan component for operating or bridging needs.

### How do I find out about new agriculture grant rounds before they close?
Agriculture grant rounds are frequently only advertised for a few weeks. The best sources are your state agriculture department's newsletter, the DAFF website at agriculture.gov.au, and your relevant industry body (e.g., Australian Pork, Dairy Australia, Hort Innovation, Australian Wool Innovation).
